8. 
**When can a player change wet or damaged uniforms?** Between sets or after a substitution, provided the new uniform is the same colour and design.


9. 
**What objects are forbidden for players to wear?** Objects that may cause injury or give an artificial advantage.


10. 
**Under what condition can an Assistant Coach assume the coach's functions?** Should the coach have to leave for any reason, including sanctions.



Topic: Playing Format and Rules

11. 
**Define a "Completed Rally."** It is the sequence of playing actions which results in the award of a point.


12. 
**How is the "Default" score recorded?** 0-3 for the match and 0-25 for each set.


13. 
**Describe the "Positional Fault."** It occurs if a player is not in their correct position at the moment the ball is hit by the server.


14. 
**What determines the positions of players on the floor?** The positions of their feet contacting the ground.


15. 
**What is a "Double Fault"?** When two or more faults are committed by opponents simultaneously; the rally is replayed.



Topic: Playing Actions

16. 
**When is the ball considered "Out of Play"?** At the moment of the fault whistled by a referee; or at the moment of the whistle in the absence of a fault.


17. 
**What is an "Assisted Hit"?** A player taking support from a teammate or any structure to hit the ball within the playing area.


18. 
**Explain the "Four Hits" fault.** A team hits the ball four times before returning it.


19. 
**What are the rules regarding "Penetration under the net"?** It is permitted provided it does not interfere with the opponent’s play.


20. 
**Define a "Completed Block."** A block is completed whenever the ball is touched by a blocker.



Topic: The Libero

21. 
**Can a Libero perform an attack hit?** No, if at the moment of contact the ball is entirely higher than the top of the net.


22. 
**What is a "Libero Replacement"?** The act by which the Libero replaces a back-row player.


23. 
**Is there a limit to Libero replacements?** No, they are unlimited.


24. 
**How must a Libero's uniform differ from teammates?** It must have a different dominant colour and clearly contrast with the rest of the team.


25.

Topic: Technical Definitions (Part 3)

51. 
**Define "Dribbling."** It means bouncing the ball, usually as a preparation to tossing and serving. 


52. 
**What constitutes "Fair Play"?** Participants must behave respectfully and courteously toward officials, opponents, teammates, and spectators. 


53. 
**What is the "Lower Space"?** The space defined by the bottom of the net, the cord joining it to the posts, the posts themselves, and the playing surface. 


54. 
**Define "External Space."** It is the space in the vertical plane of the net outside of the crossing and lower spaces. 


55. 
**What are "Quick Moppers"?** Personnel whose job it is to keep the floor clean and dry after each rally using small towels. 



Topic: Player Positions and Rotation (Rule 7)

56. 
**Explain the numbering of the six player positions.** Three along the net are front-row (4-left, 3-centre, 2-right); three are back-row (5-left, 6-centre, 1-right). 


57. 
**How is a "Rotational Fault" determined?** It is committed when the service is not made according to the rotational order. 


58. 
**What are the consequences of a Rotational Fault?** Opponent gains a point and next service, and the rotational order must be rectified. 


59. 
**Can the serving team occupy any position at the service hit?** Yes, they are free to occupy any position within their own court. 


60. 
**What determines a player's position at the moment of service?** The position of their feet contacting the ground. 



Topic: Specialized Refereeing Duties

61. 
**What is the primary responsibility of the "Challenge Referee"?** To supervise the challenge process and ensure it proceeds according to regulations. 


62. 
**Where does the "Reserve Referee" stand?** In a separate position determined by the FIVB court layout. 


63. 
**What does the Scorer do at the end of the match?** Records the final result, obtains signatures from captains and referees, and signs the score sheet. 


64. 
**List three responsibilities of the "Line Judges."** Signal ball "in" or "out," signal touches of "out" balls, and signal ball touching antennae. 


65. 
**What is the "Protocol"?** The series of events before the match start, including the toss, warm-up, and presentation of teams. 


Topic: Game States and Hits (Rule 8-9)

66. 
**When is the ball "In Play"?** From the moment of the service hit authorized by the 1st referee. 


67. 
**What is a "Simultaneous Contact" by opponents?** When two opponents touch the ball simultaneously over the net; the team receiving the ball is entitled to another three hits. 


68. 
**Is a block contact counted as a team hit?** No, a block contact is not counted as a team hit. 


69. 
**When is an attack hit "completed"?** When the ball completely crosses the vertical plane of the net or is touched by an opponent. 


70. 
**What is the "Screening" fault?** Players of the serving team preventing the opponent from seeing the service hit and the flight path of the ball.

**What happens if a server steps on the end line during hit?** It is a service fault.

**How long is the official warm-up at the net for FIVB competitions?** 10 minutes together.

**What is the "Control Area"?** A corridor around the playing court and free zone up to the outer barriers.
